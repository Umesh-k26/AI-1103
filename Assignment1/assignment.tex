\section*{Question}
A family has two children. What is the probability that both the children are boys given that at least one of them is a boy?
\section*{Solution}
Given, a family has two children.\\
Let's denote girl by 'G' and boy by 'B'.\\
Sample space of the outcomes is given by :
$$S = [(B,B),(G,B),(B,G),(G,G)]$$
Let X denote the random variable representing number of boys.\\
$$X = \{0, 1, 2\}$$
where $n = 2$ and $p = \frac{1}{2}$
\begin{align}
    \pr{X = 0} &= {2 \choose 0}\times {q^2} &=\dfrac{1}{4}\\
    \pr{X = 1} &= {2 \choose 1}\times p \times q &=\dfrac{2}{4}\\
    \pr{X = 2} &= {2 \choose 2}\times {p^2} &=\dfrac{1}{4}\\
    \pr{X \geq 1} &= \pr{X = 1} + \pr{X = 2} &=\dfrac{3}{4}
\end{align}
\\
To find $\pr{X = 2\, | \,X \geq 1}$.
\begin{align}
    \pr{X = 2\, | \,X \geq 1} &= \dfrac{\pr{X = 2}}{\pr{X\geq 1}}\\
    &= \dfrac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{3}{4}}\\
    &= \dfrac{1}{3}
\end{align}
$\therefore$ The probability that both the children are boys given that at least one of them is a boy is $\frac{1}{3}$.
